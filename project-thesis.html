Project Overview

The Telangana Traditional Resort was the culminating thesis project of the Bachelor of Architecture program at Sri Venkateshwara College of Architecture, Hyderabad. The project proposed a resort complex that draws on the architectural traditions and material culture of the Telangana region to create a hospitality destination that is both contemporary in function and regionally rooted in character.

The resort complex comprises five distinct building types — a large banquet hall, economy cottage blocks, premium suite rooms, a full-service restaurant, and a spa with changing facilities — each designed with its own architectural response to program while maintaining a cohesive visual language derived from traditional Telangana building forms, materials, and craftsmanship.

Banquet Hall

The banquet hall (37.2m × 29.2m) is the centerpiece of the complex, designed to accommodate large gatherings with flexible seating configurations. The hall is flanked by support spaces including banquet prep areas, entry lobbies, and storage. A traditional sloped tile roof and structural column rhythm reference regional building typologies.

Economy Cottages & Suite Rooms

The economy cottage blocks provide paired rooms (5.1m × 4.75m each) arranged around a shared landscape spine. Suite rooms include private pool areas, larger room footprints, and direct landscape connections — all organized in a circular cluster arrangement visible in the aerial view.

Restaurant & Spa

The restaurant integrates indoor and outdoor dining through a deep colonnade using traditional column elements. The spa and changing room block is organized around a linear plan with separate male and female facilities, steam rooms, and relaxation areas.
