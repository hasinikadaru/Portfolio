Project Overview

The Student Housing Complex was designed for the Construction of a Minority Residential School Complex for Boys at Mahabubnagar District — a real-program project requiring structured, high-density residential accommodation with shared dining, recreation, and study facilities.

The building footprint of 52.46m × 30.08m is organized around a central spine connecting two residential wings on each floor. The symmetrical plan creates clear wayfinding while maximizing room capacity within each wing.

Ground Floor

The ground floor is organized around a large central dining hall (24,100 × 7,000) with full kitchen, service counter, and storage facilities including dry store, wet store, and cylinder store. A prayer room, warden's room, office spaces, and entry lobby complete the ground level program. Two staircases provide vertical circulation at each end of the central connector.

Upper Floors

Floors one through three follow a consistent dormitory room layout — student rooms (1000 × 5000) arranged in double-loaded corridors with shared bathroom facilities at each wing. The first floor includes a Recreation Room and Terrace above the central dining hall, creating a shared social amenity at the building's center. The second floor adds a Reading Room in the same central position.
