Project Overview

The Student Housing Complex was designed for the Construction of a Minority Residential School Complex for Boys at Mahabubnagar District — a real-program project requiring structured, high-density residential accommodation with shared dining, recreation, and study facilities across four floors.

The building footprint of 52.46m × 30.08m is organized around a central spine connecting two residential wings on each floor. The symmetrical plan creates clear wayfinding while maximizing room capacity within each wing. Two staircases at either end of the central connector provide vertical circulation throughout.

Ground Floor

The ground floor is organized around a large central dining hall (24,100 × 7,000) with full kitchen, service counter, and storage including dry store, wet store, and cylinder store. A prayer room, warden's room, office spaces, and entry lobby complete the ground level program.

Upper Floors

Floors one through three follow a consistent dormitory room layout — student rooms (1,000 × 5,000) arranged in double-loaded corridors with shared bathroom facilities at each wing. The first floor includes a Recreation Room and Terrace above the central dining hall. The second floor places a Reading Room in the same central position, maintaining a shared amenity at the building's core on each level.

Planning Approach

The design prioritizes clarity of organization and efficiency of layout — a symmetrical double-loaded corridor plan allows maximum room count per floor while maintaining legible circulation. The central spine serves as both a structural and programmatic organizing element, anchoring shared amenities (dining, recreation, reading) at the building's midpoint across each level.

Room Standardization

Dormitory rooms were standardized at 1,000 × 5,000 to allow modular repetition across all three residential floors. Shared bathroom cores are positioned at the end of each wing, minimizing plumbing runs while serving the maximum number of rooms per cluster. The consistency of the floor plate across all upper levels simplified structural coordination and reduced construction complexity for a high-density program.
