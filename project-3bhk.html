Project Overview

The Residential Flats at Alwal is a multi-unit apartment building on a corner site bounded by 40'-wide roads on two sides. The building accommodates two 3BHK units per floor across four floors, totaling eight residential units with shared vertical circulation via a central staircase and lift core.

Each 3BHK unit is organized around a central corridor with living, dining, and kitchen spaces oriented toward the exterior and three bedrooms — including a master bedroom with attached dressing room and toilet — arranged for privacy and natural light access on both street frontages.

Unit Planning

The first floor plan places two distinct unit types side by side, sharing a central corridor and lift core. Each unit includes a living room, dining area, kitchen with utility, pooja room, master bedroom with dressing and toilet, two additional bedrooms each with attached toilet, and a balcony. The typical floor plan consolidates this arrangement into a single efficient layout repeated on both sides of the core across all upper floors.

Planning Approach

The corner site condition was used to advantage — locating the building entry and lift core at the center of the plot allows both unit types equal access to the two street frontages, maximizing daylight and ventilation for living and bedroom spaces on opposing sides. The double-loaded corridor plan achieves high unit density without sacrificing the separation of private and shared zones within each flat.

Furniture Integration

Floor plans were drawn with full furniture layouts to test livability at the planning stage — confirming bedroom clearances, kitchen workflow, and living room proportions before finalizing the unit configuration. This approach was applied consistently across the first floor, typical floor, and third floor plans to verify the design at each level.
